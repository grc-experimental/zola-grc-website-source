Ruo-Chen Gu

Master's student in mathematics, UW–Madison

Definition (Ruo-Chen Gu).

Born in Shanghai and now based in Madison, Ruo-Chen Gu is a master's student in mathematics whose center of gravity lies in mathematical logic and automated theorem proving. He also keeps active interests in analysis, algebra, and geometry/topology—broad enough to navigate the major classical areas, but with logic and ATP as the main filled dot.

● logic / ATP   ○ analysis  ○ algebra  ○ geometry/topology

Background.

Before entering mathematics properly, he completed a double major in finance and computer science at New York University. The combination turned out to be surprisingly natural preparation for foundations: computational instincts from CS and the skepticism trained by quantitative work both fed into a long-term interest in rigor, formal systems, and the possibility of delegating parts of mathematical reasoning to machines without losing its conceptual core.

Experience.

In 2025 he attended the SLMath Summer Graduate School on Computer-Assisted Proof in Applied Mathematics, where exposure to rigorous numerics and formal verification strengthened his interest in the interface between analysis and machine-checked reasoning.

Research direction.

He is steadily building toward doctoral work in foundations, with an emphasis on how rigorous classical mathematics interacts with formal verification and proof assistants. His working heuristic is simple: if a proof can be made precise, then eventually it should be precise enough for a machine to check—without ceasing to be mathematics.
